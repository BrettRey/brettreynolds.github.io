# Deitality Journal Strategy
Date: 2026-06-06
## Recommendation
Yes, send the deitality paper to a journal, but do not send it in its current project state without a short submission-prep pass. My recommended first target is **Journal of Linguistics**.

The paper has a real publishable hook: it coins and motivates `deitality` as a structural category distinct from semantic definiteness, then uses a homeostatic-property-cluster framework to explain why the two correlate but fail to coincide. That is original enough to deserve journal review. The issue is venue positioning, not whether the paper is worth sending.
## Current Submission Load
The relevant active queue is crowded:

- **English Language and Linguistics**: `English determinatives as nouns`

- **Linguistics**: `The homeostatic maintenance of English countability`

- **Glossa**: `Focus modification and interrogative heads`

- **Canadian Journal of Linguistics/Revue canadienne de linguistique**: reciprocals/boundary phenomena

- **Functions of Language**: operator-stratum grammaticality paper

- **Journal of Pragmatics**: English interjections

- **Journal of English Linguistics**: English kinship terms

- **Folia Linguistica**: personhood and pro-forms

- **Language Sciences**, **Mind & Language**, **Philosophy and the Mind Sciences**, **Journal of Social Ontology**, and others also have active submissions.


Two simultaneous submissions to one journal are not inherently impossible. The strategic risk is different: if the same editor sees multiple closely adjacent English-grammar/HPC papers at once, it can look like topical crowding or salami-slicing even when the papers are genuinely distinct.
## Ranked Targets
### 1. Journal of Linguistics
This is my first choice. The journal says it covers all branches of theoretical linguistics and prefers original research articles based on sound empirical work that make a clear contribution to current theoretical debate. That matches the strongest version of this paper: a theoretically consequential analysis of the English definiteness/deitality split, grounded in diagnostics such as existential `there`, partitives, identificational hosting, and `one`-substitution.

Advantages:

- no current paper is under submission there;

- you already have a successful publication record with the journal;

- it avoids crowding ELL and Linguistics while still reaching theoretical linguists;

- the paper can be pitched as syntax-semantics-interface work rather than only as an English-description article.


Submission positioning:

- title/abstract should foreground the linguistic problem before the HPC machinery;

- cover letter should say the paper contributes a new structural category at the syntax-semantics interface;

- reduce any sense that the article is mainly philosophy-of-science exposition.

### 2. English Language and Linguistics
This is the best content fit, but not the best immediate strategic fit. ELL explicitly covers all core aspects of English, including syntax, semantics, pragmatics, lexis, and the structure/development of English within contemporary linguistic theory. Deitality is exactly an English structure paper.

The problem is queue optics. `English determinatives as nouns` is already under review at ELL, and the deitality paper also concerns English determiners/determination. Submitting both concurrently is defensible, but I would avoid doing so unless:

- ELL returns a quick decision on the determinatives paper;

- Journal of Linguistics desk rejects on fit;

- or you decide that ELL fit matters more than diversification.

### 3. Glossa
Glossa is a credible alternative if the paper is sharpened as general-linguistic theory. Its stated scope is broad general linguistics, with theoretical implications for the nature of language and the language faculty. The paper would need to make the generality of `deitality` very clear, not read as an English-only taxonomy.

I would wait for the pending Glossa focus-modifier decision if possible, since local tracking expects movement around 2026-06-20. Also check length: Glossa initial submissions should not exceed 13,000 words including footnotes and references. The generated Markdown is about 11,600 words, so the paper is probably close but not automatically safe once formatted.
### 4. Journal of Semantics / Semantics and Pragmatics
These are tempting because the topic is definiteness, but they are not the best current-form targets. Journal of Semantics covers theoretical meaning in natural language, including pragmatics and the syntax-semantics interface, with emphasis on formal and experimental methods. Semantics and Pragmatics asks for new results significant for theoretical development in semantics/pragmatics.

The paper could go this route only after a heavier rewrite:

- formalize the semantic alternatives more explicitly;

- make definiteness, weak definites, generic definites, and proper names the centre;

- move the HPC machinery into a supporting role;

- add a clearer contribution to the formal semantics/pragmatics literature.


Without that rewrite, a semantics journal may see the paper as too grammatical/theory-of-categories oriented.
### 5. Linguistics
Linguistics is a plausible fit because it covers grammatical organization and meaning/use in human language. But `countability` is already under review there, and the two papers are close in brand: English grammar category + HPC + projectibility. I would not send deitality there while countability is pending unless another target fails.
### Lower-Priority Backups
- **Journal of English Linguistics**: good English-language scope, but you already have kinship terms under review there, and JEL leans toward empirical English linguistics. Deitality would need more data-driven framing.

- **Canadian Journal of Linguistics**: broad enough, but reciprocals is already there and the fit is less direct than JL/ELL.

- **Functions of Language**: possible only if reframed heavily around discourse-pragmatic function; operator-stratum is already there.

- **Natural Language & Linguistic Theory**: not a good first target unless rewritten in a much more formal generative syntax style.

## Before Submission
Do a short but real prep pass before sending anywhere:

1. Keep `Claude.tex` as canonical, not `main.tex`.

2. Make the build workflow target `Claude.tex` cleanly.

3. Re-check the project STATUS blockers, because at least some are stale: `Claude.pdf` exists, and the old inline query at line 178 no longer appears there.

4. Reconcile any relevant Kim feedback deliberately; do not assume the old `main.tex` branch already represents the current paper.

5. Make the title/abstract less likely to sound like a philosophy-first paper. Something like **Definiteness is not deitality: Structural and semantic clustering in English determination** may be more journal-facing.

6. Add the AI-use disclosure in the submission form or title page, not in the anonymous manuscript body, according to the target journal's policy.

7. Prepare a blind version and a short cover letter that explicitly distinguishes this paper from the currently submitted determinatives/countability papers.

## Bottom Line
Submit it, but send it first to **Journal of Linguistics** after a focused polish. If JL rejects on fit, send to **English Language and Linguistics**, preferably after the current ELL determinatives paper has moved. Use **Glossa** as the open-access/general-theory alternative, especially if the pending Glossa squib decision clears first.
