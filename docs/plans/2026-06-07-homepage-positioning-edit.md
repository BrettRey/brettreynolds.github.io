# Homepage Positioning Edit

Date: 2026-06-07

## Recommendation

Yes: modify the homepage, but lightly. The current homepage is accurate, but it still presents the work mainly as English grammar plus syntactic annotation plus grammaticality. That undersells the broader through-line we just named: **category stability and inference**.

The goal should be to add that umbrella without making the homepage sound like a career pivot or like a philosophy slogan.

## Proposed Changes

### Meta Description

Current:

> Brett Reynolds - Linguist at Humber Polytechnic and University of Toronto. Research on English grammar, syntactic annotation, and grammaticality.

Proposed:

> Brett Reynolds - Linguist at Humber Polytechnic and University of Toronto. Research on English grammar, syntactic annotation, and category stability and inference.

### Bio

Current opening research sentence:

> His work centres on English grammar and grammatical categories, with interests in syntactic annotation, constructionist/usage-based analysis, the philosophy of linguistics, and what grammaticality judgements can – and can't – tell us.

Proposed replacement:

> His work asks how useful but imperfect categories hold together, what they let us infer, and where those inferences break down. In linguistics, this means English grammar and grammatical categories: syntactic annotation, usage-based and constructionist analysis, the philosophy of linguistics, and what grammaticality judgements can – and can't – tell us.

### Research Focus Line

Current:

> Research focus: English grammar, grammatical categories, syntactic annotation, usage-based/constructionist analysis, philosophy of linguistics, grammaticality judgments.

Proposed:

> Research focus: category stability and inference; English grammar and grammatical categories; syntactic annotation; usage-based/constructionist analysis; philosophy of linguistics; grammaticality judgments.

## Why This Version

It makes the homepage more accurate to the current research programme without leading with "HPC theory" or "projectibility." It also keeps the public homepage rooted in linguistics, which is still the cleanest identity signal for readers arriving from publications, talks, or search.

I would not add AI evaluation to the homepage sentence yet. It is better to let that show up through publications and future pages unless the homepage is being repositioned for job-search or consulting audiences.
